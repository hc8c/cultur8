About Criteria Join

Why join or use this web chain (web ring)?

Simply put, because it's getting harder and harder to discover original, human-created content online.

Search engine services, and their AI derivatives, are largely geared to providing one-stop answers to simple or specific questions, and less so about browsing, "surfing," or stumbling across interesting content within a particular niche.

As a result, rather than pools of diverse, organic and free-flowing content, the web is now dominated by content that's been written to rank highly in search engines, to serve advertising, or to grab user data. Sure, it's much easier to get fast answers to trivia, or find practical how-to guides, but much harder to find rabbit holes of fascinating content, never mind disappear into them. Hopefully, that's what this web ring will offer.

I've used onionring to get this started, with thanks to the original creators.

Who should join?

The intention is to curate and link independent sites that aren't already served by SEO, ad networks, large funding, etc. There's no cost to join the chain; the only requirements are detailed below.

Requirements

  • Blogs, websites, self-hosted or on community pages: all are welcome. They may be written by a single person or several, but the site's overall ownership should be individuals or collectives, not companies. This applies to the editorial outlook, too. This isn't the place for advertising or product placement masquering as a blog.
  • For now, content should be in English. This is simply to make it easier to review sites before they're added to this web chain.
  • Sites should offer original commentary, analysis or theories of film, TV or books. It can be any or all of those art forms, but should include at least one as a named category on your site.
  • The site's webmaster, whether that's the owner, a writer, developer etc, must have the ability to add a html snippet to the site's footer, as this is how the web chain's navigation will work. Adding the widget to your site, and ensuring it stays in place, is a necessary part of chain membership.

Not currently accepting:

  • Sites that predominantly or only offer reviews rather than detailed analysis. (Nothing against these, just that these are already easier to discover.)
  • Absolutely no AI generated, structured or suggested articles. The sites in this chain should reflect the beauty of human thought and endeavour.
  • Content mustn't be locked behind paywalls, restrictive cookie notices, or notices that force users to turn off adblockers.

Please nominate your favourite sites!
