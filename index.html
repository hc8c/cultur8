Prerving the human web

Simply put, it's getting harder to discover original, human-created content online.

Search engines, and their AI derivatives, are largely geared to providing one-stop answers to simple or specific questions, and less so about browsing, "surfing," or stumbling across interesting content within a particular niche.

As a result, rather than pools of diverse, organic and free-flowing content, the web is now dominated by content that's been written to rank highly in search engines, serve advertising, sell you stuff, or grab your personal data.

Sure, it's much easier to get fast answers to trivia, or find practical how-to guides, but much harder to find rabbit holes of fascinating content, never mind disappear into them. Hopefully, that's what this web chain will offer. Essentially, it's a collection of links to cool content humans have written and published online, and which are at risk of vanishing from view.

How does a web chain work?

Sites that are part of a particular web chain display a small nav bar. Visitors can use the "next", "prev" and "random" buttons to visit other sites in the chain. It's beautifully simple.

I've used onionring to get this started, with thanks to the original creators.

Who should join this web chain?

The intention is to curate and link independent sites that aren't already served by SEO, ad networks, corporate funding, etc. There's no cost to join the chain; the only requirements are detailed below.

Sites and content

  • This web chain is for sites that produce written content. How you host or style that content is up to you. Blogs, websites, self-hosted, or on community pages, coded from scratch or from templates: all are welcome.
  • Content may be authored by a single person or several, but the site should be owned and operated by individuals or collectives, not companies, agencies or businesses. This isn't the place or product placement masquering as content.
  • For now, content should be in English. This is simply to make it easier to review sites before they're added to this web chain.
  • Sites should offer original commentary, analysis or theories of film, TV or books. It can be any or all of those art forms, but should include at least one as a named category on your site.

Webmasters

  • The site's webmaster, whether that's the owner, a writer, developer etc, must be able to add a html snippet to their site, as this is how visitors will travel around the chain.
  • Adding the widget to your site, and keeping it and your site active, are necessary for continued membership of the chain.

Not currently accepting

  • Sites that predominantly or only offer reviews or news rather than detailed analysis. (Nothing against these, just that there already are ways to discover this kind of content.)
  • Sites that use AI generated, structured or suggested content. The sites in this chain should reflect the possibilities of human thought and endeavour.
  • Content mustn't be locked behind paywalls, restrictive cookie notices, or anti-adblock tech.
