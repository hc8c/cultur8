Cultur8

A collection of sites about culture commentary (Film, TV, books)

Why make this content chain (web ring)?

Simply put, because it's getting harder and harder to discover original, human-created content online.

Search engine services, and their AI derivatives, are largely geared around providing one-stop answers to simple or specific questions, and less so about browsing, "surfing," or stumbling across interesting content within a particular niche.

As a result, rather than pools of diverse, organic and free-flowing content, the web is now dominated by content that's been written to rank highly in search engines, to serve advertising, or to grab user data. Sure, it's much easier to get fast answers to trivia, or find practical how-to guides, but much harder to find rabbit holes of fascinating content, never mind disappear into them. Hopefully, that's what this web ring will offer.

Credit: I've used open source templates by onionring to get this going. Thank you to the original creators!

Which sites are invited to join?

Here's the current criteria for sites. The intention is to curate sites that aren't already served by SEO, ad networks, etc.

  • For now, sites should be written in English. This makes it easier to review content before it's added to the creator chain (it may change later on).
  • Sites should offer original commentary, analysis or theories of film, TV or books. It can be any or all of those art forms, but should include at least one as a named category on your site.
  • Content shouldn't predominantly or only offer reviews.
  • Content should be written by humans: no AI generated, structured or suggested articles. The sites on this list should reflect the wonders of human thought.
  • This isn't the place for sites that are locked behind paywalls, restrictive cookie notices, or that force users to turn off adblockers.
