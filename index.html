Fliterati

:: Awesome indie sites serving culture commentary ::

("Fliterati", noun, portmanteau of film & literati)

About Criteria Join

Why join or use a web chain (web ring)?

Simply put, because it's getting harder to discover original, human-created content online.

Search engines, and their AI derivatives, are largely geared to providing one-stop answers to simple or specific questions, and less so about browsing, "surfing," or stumbling across interesting content within a particular niche.

As a result, rather than pools of diverse, organic and free-flowing content, the web is now dominated by content that's been written to rank highly in search engines, to serve advertising, or to grab user data. Sure, it's much easier to get fast answers to trivia, or find practical how-to guides, but much harder to find rabbit holes of fascinating content, never mind disappear into them. Hopefully, that's what this web ring will offer.

I've used onionring to get this started, with thanks to the original creators.

Who should join?

The intention is to curate and link independent sites that aren't already served by SEO, ad networks, corporate funding, etc. There's no cost to join the chain; the only requirements are detailed below.

Sites and content

  • Blogs, websites, self-hosted, or on community pages, coded from scratch or templates all the way: all are welcome.
  • Sites may be authored by a single person or several, but the site's ownership, management and editorial direction should be directed by individuals or collectives, not companies or agencies. This isn't the place for ad-heavy projects or product placement masquering as a blog.
  • For now, content should be in English. This is simply to make it easier to review sites before they're added to this web chain.
  • Sites should offer original commentary, analysis or theories of film, TV or books. It can be any or all of those art forms, but should include at least one type as a named category on your site.

Webmasters

  • The site's webmaster, whether that's the owner, a writer, developer etc, must be able to add a html snippet to the site's footer, as this is how visitors will travel between sites in the chain.
  • As the site grows, webmasters will collectively decide new members (i.e., by vote).
  • Adding the widget to your site, and keeping it and your site active, are necessary for continued membership of the chain.

Not currently accepting:

  • Sites that predominantly or only offer reviews rather than detailed analysis. (Nothing against these, just that there already are ways to discover this kind of content.)
  • Absolutely no AI generated, structured or suggested articles. The sites in this chain should reflect the possibilities of human thought and endeavour.
  • Content mustn't be locked behind paywalls, restrictive cookie notices, or anti-adblock tech. If you already have these, you probably don't need a web chain.
